04, Investment Strategy
Three Scenarios.
One Unified Book.
01
Put Skew Harvesting
Structural short put skew in front months captures the persistent hedging premium priced into WTI/Brent downside puts. IV in OTM puts consistently exceeds realized volatility, creating a reliable theta-to-gamma edge in sideways and mean-reverting environments.

Term structure positioning: Front-Short / Back-Flat maintains vega-compression protection and minimizes parallel surface shift risk.
P&L Driver: Theta Decay / Skew Compression
02
Dynamic Downside Defense
As spot approaches short-put strikes, the desk executes contingent delta/vega flattening, buying back exposure and increasing long gamma to prevent "short gamma into a falling knife" scenarios.

This Defensive Rebalancing Protocol ensures the 10/20/30% stress test parameters are maintained through sharp liquidation events, keeping max drawdown within defined limits.
P&L Driver: Gamma / Vega Rebalancing
03
Right-Tail Convexity
Unlike equity volatility, crude oil is subject to right-tail explosions, geopolitical conflict, pipeline failures, OPEC+ surprises. The desk maintains long call wing and back-month exposure specifically to capture these events.

When a supply shock hits, spot price and implied volatility expand simultaneously, creating double-convexity gains. This is the portfolio's highest-asymmetry position and the primary driver of the 200–300%+ return scenarios.
P&L Driver: Right-Tail Convexity / Vol Explosion
